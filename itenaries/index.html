  • DURATION: 6 days

  • ELEVATION: NA

  • PACKAGE INCLUDES: Kigali city Tour, Kigali Genocide memorial, Richard Kandt Museum and Mount Kigali.

Day 1: Arrive in Rwanda
Upon landing in Rwanda, your driver guide will be waiting for you at the airport exit, meet you, share a short briefing and transfer you to your booked hotel in Kigali, for dinner and an overnight stay at Hotel des Mille Collines. Meals: dinner

Day 2: Transfer to Nyungwe
Have breakfast at your hotel in Kigali in the morning, you will be met by your safari guide, who will warmly welcome you, share a short briefing with you, and later, you hit the road to Nyungwe, with a beautiful countryside view. Along the way, you will visit the King Palace Museum, learn more about the Rwanda culture and later proceed with your drive to Nyungwe with an en-route lunch. Reach in the afternoon, head for Gisakura tea tour, to learn about tea processing and farming. Dinner and overnight at Nyungwe Top View Lodge.

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & dinner.

Day 3: Chimpanzee trekking and canopy walk.
Wake up early, have breakfast meet your guide, and transfer for the chimpanzee trekking, where you will meet your ranger guides and head for the trekking in the forest. The trekking often last for 1 – 6 hours depending on the chimpanzee’s movements. You will later return and have lunch before heading for a canopy walk experience on the 70 m suspended metallic bridge, which is roughly an hour’s walk. Return to hotel for dinner and overnight at Nyungwe Top View Lodge

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & dinner.

Day 4: Transfer to Volcanoes National Park
Have early morning breakfast, meet your guide and jump on your jeep, find your way to Volcanoes, via Karongi, for a boat ride on Lake Kivu, to explore the beautiful islands, with lots of bird species before you hit the road and proceed to Volcanoes National Park, with an en route lunch. You will reach in the evening and check in at your booked accommodation for dinner and an overnight stay at Ingagi Park View Lodge. Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & dinner.

Day 5: Gorilla trekking experience
After having your early morning breakfast at 6:30 am, you will meet your guide and transfer to Volcanoes National Park, for gorilla group allocation, briefing and later drive to starting point and head for your gorilla trekking, where you will meet the gorillas and an hour will be allocated to you upon finding the gorillas. This is an amazing experience, referred to as a lifetime experience by most clients. Later return, head for lunch and after lunch, you have an optional visit to the Ellen DeGeneres Campus of Iby’Iwacu cultural center. Dinner and overnight stay at Ingagi Park View Lodge. Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & dinner.

Day 6: Golden Monkey trekking experience and transfer to Kigali
After having your early morning breakfast, your guide will be ready waiting for you, and transfer you to the park, for your golden monkey trekking adventure. The same procedures as gorilla trekking are followed for the golden monkey trekking, though golden monkey trekking is less strenuous as compared to gorilla trekking. Return to have lunch and transfer to Kigali, and catch up with your evening flight back home. Meals: breakfast and lunch

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION

  • What currency is used in Rwanda?

    Rwandan Franc (RWF)

  • What time zone is Rwanda in?

    Rwanda shares the same time zone as the rest of Southern Africa, which is two hours ahead of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) +2 hours, in the Central Africa Time Zone (CAT)

  • Can I use my credit cards in Rwanda?

    Yes, MasterCard and Visa are usually accepted throughout Rwanda, including Wilderness destinations. Unfortunately we are unable to accept American Express and Diners Club cards at our Wilderness properties.

  • What do I pack for a safari in Rwanda?

    There may be luggage weight or size restrictions on your journey – so please check the details with your Travel Designer. While Rwanda has no strict dress code, women travellers are advised to dress conservatively. Warm clothing is recommended for the cooler months, and light clothing for summer. Bright colours and white are not advisable on safari.

  • What to see in Rwanda?

    Rwanda is a land of staggering beauty with extraordinary biodiversity, iconic wildlife, volcanoes, montane rainforest and sweeping plains. Discover our areas by boat or 4x4 vehicle in Akagera National Park. Gorilla trekking is a must-do, as well as golden monkey trekking in Volcanoes National Park. Besides the rich wildlife, you can also explore Kigali, the bustling capital city, alive with tradition and culture. The country has many other exceptional national parks, natural forests and volcanic lakes, including Lake Kivu, one of Africa's Great Lakes.

  • Why should I visit Rwanda?

    Rwanda is famous for its mountain gorillas, birdwatching, classic East African safari encounters, cultural experiences, hiking, and our award-winning luxury camps. All set in exclusive, breath-taking regions of Rwanda, ideal for discovering the country’s iconic wildlife.
